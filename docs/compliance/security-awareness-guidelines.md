## 1. Purpose

These guidelines help employees understand their role in protecting [COMPANY_NAME]'s information and systems. Security is everyone's responsibility. Complete security awareness training as required by policy (minimum [ANNUALLY]).

---

## 2. Security Principles Overview

- **Confidentiality**: Protect sensitive information from unauthorized access or disclosure.
- **Integrity**: Ensure information and systems are accurate and not improperly modified.
- **Availability**: Support reliable access to systems and data for authorized use.

Follow [COMPANY_NAME]'s [Security Policy](security-policy.md) and [Data Handling Policy](data-handling-policy.md) in your daily work.

---

## 3. Password and Authentication Best Practices

- Use a strong, unique password for work systems (per [Security Policy](security-policy.md)).
- Do not share passwords or reuse work passwords elsewhere.
- Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) where required; do not bypass it.
- Do not store passwords in plain text (e.g., in email, spreadsheets, or sticky notes).
- Report suspected compromise of your credentials immediately to [IT/Security contact].

---

## 4. Phishing and Social Engineering Awareness

- Be cautious of unexpected or urgent requests for information, credentials, or payments.
- Verify the sender (check email addresses and links) before clicking or replying.
- Do not provide credentials, personal data, or sensitive data via email or unknown links.
- Report suspected phishing to [CONTACT_EMAIL] or your IT/Security team; do not forward to broad distribution without guidance.
- When in doubt, contact the purported sender through a known, trusted channel.

---

## 5. Data Handling Responsibilities

- Handle data according to its classification (see [Data Handling Policy](data-handling-policy.md)).
- Store and transmit confidential data only via approved, secure methods (e.g., encrypted channels, approved apps).
- Do not send confidential or personal data to personal email or unapproved cloud storage.
- Follow retention and disposal procedures; do not retain data longer than necessary.
- Report loss, theft, or unauthorized disclosure of sensitive data immediately.

---

## 6. Incident Reporting Procedures

Report the following to [CONTACT_EMAIL] or [SECURITY_CONTACT_NAME] as soon as possible:

- Suspected or actual unauthorized access to systems or data
- Lost or stolen devices or credentials
- Malware or suspicious activity on your device or in systems
- Phishing or social engineering attempts (for awareness and response)
- Physical security concerns (e.g., unauthorized visitors, unsecured areas)

Do not attempt to hide or downplay incidents; early reporting supports containment and compliance.

See [Incident Response Plan](incident-response-plan.md) for organizational procedures.

---

## 7. Acceptable Use Reminders

- Use [COMPANY_NAME] systems and data only for authorized business purposes.
- Do not install unapproved software or use unauthorized external services for work data.
- Do not use work assets for illegal or inappropriate activities.
- Respect intellectual property and confidentiality of [COMPANY_NAME] and third parties.
- Remote access and mobile devices must comply with [COMPANY_NAME] policy (e.g., lock screens, encryption, reporting loss).
